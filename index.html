Why wellness

This isn't a niche for me. It's how I live.

Outside of work, wellness is the whole thing for me. I'm the friend who reads the labels, tries the protocol first, and goes deep on whatever's coming next. I consider myself an early adopter, always looking for ways to optimize my health and limit what I'm exposed to.

Last year I was diagnosed with Hashimoto's, and instead of treating it as a footnote, I let it pull me all the way in. Through a lot of research, a lot of small daily changes, and a real commitment to my own health, I was able to get my Hashimoto's into remission.

That experience sharpened my instinct for what people searching for health support are really going through.

It's why I have such a strong pulse on this space, what's working, what's coming next, and what truly matters to someone who's just trying to feel better. When the thing you market all day is the thing you care about most, the work stops feeling like marketing and starts feeling like help.

A type A planner who genuinely loves the plan.

Give me the goal and I'll build the plan, the content, and the reporting that proves it worked. I'm organized to a fault and I manage my time well, which is how I've handled whole marketing functions on my own without anything slipping.

  • ✦ I keep a clear calendar and defined KPIs, so everyone always knows what's shipping and why.
